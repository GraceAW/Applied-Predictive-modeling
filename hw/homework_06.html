Problem 01¶

The first synthetic data involves a single categorical input. One input is used to support visualization and to easily display results to the screen. Even though the data may be fake, this style of problem is quite common in many fields. As an example, the last assignment introduced you to the UCI Machine Learning Repository Concrete Data Set. The goal of that application is to predict the strength of concrete based on various components that create the concrete mixture. The strength was measured from samples produced in experiments and trials. The strength might have been measured in different labs by different people at different times. The measurements must be conducted in a consistent manner with similar equipment or there will be no way to use all measurements to train machine learning models! The models are only as good as the data that train them! If you cannot trust the measurements then you cannot trust the models!

Why does this matter? Laboratories are routinely involved in repeability studies. Different labs are provided with samples of material. The labs measure the material using their standard operating procedures. The results are then shared and the measurements compared across the labs. The goal is to identify if the different labs produce measurements that are within the margin of error of each other. Data from these studies can be organized where the lab is a categorical input and the measurement of interest is the response. Therefore, the lab repeability study can be viewed as a linear model for regression with a single categorical input and single continuous response!

You will use the "fake" lab repeability study to practice working with categorical inputs for linear models.
